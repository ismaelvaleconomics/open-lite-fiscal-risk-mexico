# Model overview

This document provides a conceptual overview of the Open Lite fiscal-risk model.

## Objective

The model studies the relationship between fiscal consolidation, public debt, and sovereign risk premia in a small open economy.

The central mechanism is:

Fiscal consolidation → Higher primary balance → Lower debt → Lower risk premium → Exchange rate appreciation → Lower inflation → Monetary policy adjustment.

## Key parameters

The most important parameter in the model is:

$k_{rp}$

which captures the sensitivity of the sovereign risk premium to public debt.

A higher value implies stronger financial market reactions to fiscal conditions.

## Policy experiments

The model can simulate:

• spending-based fiscal consolidations  
• revenue-based fiscal consolidations  
• sovereign risk shocks  
• external interest rate shocks  

## Interpretation

The framework is intended as a parsimonious analytical model to understand debt–risk interactions rather than a fully estimated DSGE model.
